Japanese Calligraphy or Shodo 書道

In the 5th Century, Shodo a form of Japanese art appeared. Shodo translates to ​"the way of artistic hand writing or beautiful writing"(site src).This is what Japanese Calligraphy is called. The overall history of various forms of calligraphy are very cool, many cultures containing them in their history. In Japan, Shodo is considered and anesthetic and skill. Japanese people also have a very high bar and regard set for Shodo. There are various techniques used to achieve beautiful pieces of Shodo. Characters in Japanese are called Kanji. The Kanji for Shodo is 書道 . 書 means writing in Japanese. 道 means the way in Japanese.Shodo is meant to be simple and an important thing is to have a mind-body connection. People who have no mind and can relax easily find Shodo easy, in Japanese this is called MUSHIN 無心. Shodo can also be seen as a form of meditation, the artist being able to relax drawing and writing Kanji. Doing Shodo is suppose to be relaxing and lead you to a peaceful mind.
