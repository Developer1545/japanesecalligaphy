Japanese Calligraphy or Shodo 書道

Shodo translates to ​"the way of artistic hand writing or beautiful writing"(site src).This is what Japanese Calligraphy is called. The overall history of various forms of calligraphy are very cool, many cultures containing them in their history. In Japan, Shodo is considered and anesthetic and skill. Japanese people also have a very high bar and regard set for Shodo. There are various techniques used to achieve beautiful pieces of Shodo. Characters in Japanese are called Kanji. The Kanji for Shodo is 書道 . 書 means writing in Japanese. 道 means the way in Japanese.Shodo is meant to be simple and an important thing is to have a mind-body connection. People who have no mind and can relax easily find Shodo easy, in Japanese this is called MUSHIN 無心. Shodo can also be seen as a form of meditation, the artist being able to relax drawing and writing Kanji. Doing Shodo is suppose to be relaxing and lead you to a peaceful mind.

Creators and Types

Ono no Michikaze

Created by Ono no Michikaze. He was a poet, government official, and calligrapher. It was brought over from China in the time around the 5th and 6th centuries. Shodo is still practiced today in Japan and in art classes around the world. Shodo is an art form, a way of communication, and a way of relaxing. There are three styles of Shodo, Kaisho, Gyosho, and Sosho. Kaisho is what people learn with. It is very block like and emphazizes correctness. "Kai" translates to correctness. Gyosho is less formal than Kaisho, and it flows better and is less blocky. Strokes are supposed to be fluid and flowing. Finally Sosho is the most difficult to do and understand. It is a cursive style and is meant to flow. Energy is supposed to be translated into your work.

Tools and writing styles

Japanese Brush

The most important thing to Shodo is the brush or Fude(筆). There are two types of brushes, a Hosofude(細筆) or fine brush and a Futofude(太筆)or thick brush. Originally, both were made with bamboo and animal furs. Second you need ink or Sumi. Early inks were made of materials like graphite, but now they are made of pine branch soot. In Japan, Nara and Suzuka have very fine and good Sumi. Next, you need paper. The Japanese use Mulberry Paper or Washi(和紙). This paper is stronger than normal printer or notebook paper and it absorbs more ink. Finally, you need an inkstone or Suzuri(硯)to rub the ink on.
